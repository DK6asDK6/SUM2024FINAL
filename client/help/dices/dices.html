Dark forest, where you were thrown by hand of your God, is always dangerous. Going througth you came across a lonely wolf. His gray fur is stained with blood, but it is ready to fight. Initiative rolls!
Wait, rolls? But how?


The answer is easy. DK6Dragon's dungeon supports dice throwing by using ".roll" function.
The syntax is easy. Let's take an example:
.roll 1d20:INTI
First, we initiate .roll command. Then, we mention count of throws and dice value (in D&D we use d2 (optional), d4, d6, d10, d12 and d20 dices). If you want, you can mention characteristics to add to throws using :'characteristics' mark as swown in example. What is also important, you do not really have to type characteristics bonus in uppercase, so your command can look like this:
.roll 3d8:dex
